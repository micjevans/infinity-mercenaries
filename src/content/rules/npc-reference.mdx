Some contracts call for non-player troopers that act on their own. Use this reference when a mission introduces hostile NPCs, neutral actors, mobs, monsters, or other automated units.

## Activating an NPC

<RuleCard eyebrow="Procedure" title="NPC Activation Sequence">
  <ol>
    <li>NPCs activate before Player 1's turn unless otherwise specified.</li>
    <li>If there are multiple NPCs, activate them clockwise, starting from Player 1's Deployment Zone and continuing around the table.</li>
    <li>An NPC performs all of its available Orders before moving to the next NPC.</li>
    <li>When an NPC activates, it first performs a Movement Short Skill.</li>
    <li>Players then declare AROs.</li>
    <li>Finally, draw a card from the NPC's deck to determine its second Short Skill.</li>
  </ol>
</RuleCard>

### Unconscious Troopers

By default, NPCs do not target Unconscious troopers unless all other troopers are also Unconscious.

### Movement

NPCs always move according to their traits, whether the movement is their first Short Skill, second Short Skill, Dodge, or another movement effect. Each NPC has at least one Movement trait, though some have several traits that further refine how they move.

### Facing

At the end of any Order, whether their own or a player's, NPCs orient their facing perpendicular to the target of their most recent interaction. If there was no interaction, they face perpendicular to their most recent movement.

## Second Short Skill

<RuleCard eyebrow="Priority List" title="Choosing an Active Skill">
  <p>Each NPC has a priority list of Skills and weapons. Read down the list and use the first option the NPC can legally use. Skip anything marked ARO Only.</p>
  <p>By default, an NPC tries to target as many targets as possible by splitting Burst evenly or placing templates to affect as many targets as it can.</p>
  <p>When activating outside of ARO, NPCs ignore Long Skill restrictions. For example, if an NPC has Grenades or another Speculative weapon, it can perform a Speculative Attack if it has no other valid targets, as if it had not moved.</p>
</RuleCard>

### Generated AROs

Players can only generate AROs against the first Movement Skill an NPC trooper performs. Even if the drawn card or second Skill would normally generate additional AROs, it does not.

### Backup

If no Skill can be selected from the list, or all generated AROs are left uncontested, the NPC Dodges or Resets. If there are no AROs to contest, the NPC takes a second Move using its second MOV value. This second Move generates no AROs.

## AROs

Each NPC has a priority list of Skills and weapons for AROs. Read down the list and use the first option the NPC can legally use. During AROs, NPCs must respect normal Skill requirements, meaning they cannot place a Deployable without LoF or perform Long Skills.

In a Coordinated Order or Fireteam, NPCs always target the Spearhead or Fireteam Leader.

### Backup

If no Skill can be selected from the list, the NPC Dodges or Resets.

### Delaying and Discovering

By default, an NPC always Discovers a Camo Marker in LoF unless a trait says otherwise. When an NPC Discovers, its roll contests any action performed against it if that Marker reveals. It becomes a Face to Face Roll, but a win for the NPC simply cancels the effects of the action.
