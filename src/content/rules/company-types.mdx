Use this page to compare company types during Company creation. Each type changes how the Company is built, what bonuses it gains, and what restrictions it must follow.

Read each entry in this order:

- **Setup** tells you what kind of Company you are allowed to build.
- **Benefits** lists the special advantages that type gains.
- **Restrictions** lists the tradeoffs or limitations that come with it.

<WarningCallout title="Vanilla Armies Are Not Sectorials">
  For the purposes of all company type requirements, vanilla armies do not count as sectorials. Only named sectorials qualify. A faction's Reinforcements count as a single distinct sectorial.
</WarningCallout>

## Standard Company

<p class="company-type-tagline">The flexible all-rounder — maximum faction freedom with no tradeoffs.</p>

<CompanyTypeSection title="Setup" tone="setup">

Select any **2 sectorials** or **1 vanilla army**.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Default" tone="notes">

This is the baseline company type. It grants no extra benefits or added restrictions beyond the normal Company rules.

</CompanyTypeSection>

</CompanyTypeCard>
<CompanyTypeCard variant="cohesive">

## Cohesive Company

<p class="company-type-tagline">An elite synchronized unit built around a single fireteam's deep synergy.</p>

<CompanyTypeSection title="Setup" tone="setup">

Select any **1 sectorial** and choose one Fireteam Core available to that sectorial. The Captain and all other purchased troopers must be able to join that fireteam.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Benefits" tone="benefits">

- All fireteam members are considered the same unit for fireteam bonuses.
- This Company may form 1 Fireteam Core from its sectorial with 6 or fewer Troopers.
- Gain +1 WIP at Fireteam Level 6.
- The Captain gains **FT Master**.
- All troopers gain **Number 2**.
- Ignore the Core Fireteam's minimum trooper restriction.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Restrictions" tone="restrictions">

- Tactical Awareness may never be spent on the Core Fireteam.
- Troopers purchased with Tactical Awareness are discounted by 3 CR.
- Troopers that are not part of the Core link are considered Irregular.

</CompanyTypeSection>

</CompanyTypeCard>
<CompanyTypeCard variant="leader">

## Inspiring Leader

<p class="company-type-tagline">One exceptional Captain leading a crew of irregulars from across the Human Sphere.</p>

<CompanyTypeSection title="Setup" tone="setup">

Select any **1 sectorial** and choose a Captain from that sectorial.

Aside from the Captain, all other troopers must be Irregular troopers and may be purchased from any sectorial or vanilla army.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Benefits" tone="benefits">

The Captain gains **Inspiring Leadership**. Use any single sectorial's AVA for each trooper type without combining AVAs.

</CompanyTypeSection>

</CompanyTypeCard>
<CompanyTypeCard variant="airborne">

## Airborne Company

<p class="company-type-tagline">A hard-insertion force built entirely around Airborne Deployment. For SUPER EARTH!</p>

<CompanyTypeSection title="Setup" tone="setup">

Select any **1 sectorial** and choose a Captain from that sectorial.

Aside from the Captain, all other troopers must have an Airborne Deployment skill and may be purchased from any sectorial or vanilla army.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Benefits" tone="benefits">

The Captain gains **Parachutist** and **Network Support (Only Controlled Jump)**.

- The Company gets one free use of Speedball per contract, dropping only one Speedball.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Restrictions" tone="restrictions">

- Troopers in this Company must use an AD skill when deployed during the Deployment Phase.
- When deployed this way, troopers treat the opponent's Deployment Zone plus an additional 8 inches outside it as an Exclusion Zone.
- The Captain must be deployed during the Deployment Phase.

</CompanyTypeSection>

</CompanyTypeCard>

</CompanyTypeSection>

### TAG Profile Customization

<CompanyTypeSection title="Customization" tone="notes">

When purchased, the TAG may use **20 Spec-Ops XP** to customize the profile using the Spec-Ops equipment and skills for the Company's sectorial.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Restrictions" tone="restrictions">

- ARM, BTS, and STR may not be modified using Spec-Ops XP.
- The TAG may be the Company's Captain, but does not gain additional Spec-Ops XP.
- The TAG may not purchase Forward Deployment, Strategic Deployment, Infiltration, Impersonation, Parachutist, Combat Jump, or Engineer with Spec-Ops XP.
- The TAG may never gain those skills by any other means.

</CompanyTypeSection>

<RuleCard eyebrow="Reminder" title="Outdated Spec-Ops Skills">
  <p>
    The Spec-Ops skill list contains some outdated skills. Those outdated skills are not purchasable.
  </p>
</RuleCard>

### Upgrading and Deploying a TAG

<CompanyTypeSection title="Progression" tone="benefits">

When a TAG receives enough experience to level up, it receives a TAG Perk in addition to the normally earned perk. The TAG Perk must be chosen from the TAG Perk Tree and may not be rolled randomly.

TAGs purchase and use equipment from markets like regular troopers for all slots except **Armor**.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Deployment" tone="restrictions">

When a TAG deploys in a contract, it takes up **two slots**. This means a Company may deploy only 3 total troopers for Elite Deployment and 5 total troopers for non-Elite Deployment if the TAG is included.

</CompanyTypeSection>

## Proxy Pack

<p class="company-type-tagline">Experimental solo play: a single Lone Wolf who splits into multiple Aspects across the battlefield.</p>

<WarningCallout title="Proxy Pack Balance Warning">
Proxy Pack is poorly balanced in the current ruleset and is recommended not to be used at this time. These rules remain here for tracking and development purposes.

Players should consult their event organizer before choosing Proxy Pack as a company type.
</WarningCallout>

<CompanyTypeSection title="Setup" tone="setup">

Select any **1 sectorial or vanilla army** and choose a Captain from that force. This is the only trooper the Company may gain. The trooper does not need the Lieutenant skill, but must have Regular Training.

This trooper is the **Lone Wolf** and gains:

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Benefits" tone="benefits">

- 10 additional Spec-Ops XP, regardless of trooper cost.
- 2 Perks and 10 Renown whenever they level up.
- The ability to Doctor themselves even when not Unconscious, and to do so to regain VITA up to their maximum.
- Elite Deployment and Tactical Awareness.
- 1 Aspect at the start, 2 at Level 2, 3 at Level 3, and 4 at Level 5, to a maximum of 4 Aspects.
- The **Transfer Consciousness** skill.

</CompanyTypeSection>

### Aspects of the Wolf

<CompanyTypeSection title="Aspect Rules" tone="notes">

Instead of having multiple troopers, the Lone Wolf has multiple Aspects. Players may copy the Lone Wolf into as many trooper slots as they have Aspects and purchase/equip gear as if they were separate troopers, while keeping the underlying stats, skills, and perks.

The multiple profiles contribute to Company Renown, but the trooper's initial CR cost only counts once.

</CompanyTypeSection>

### Deploying the Wolf

<CompanyTypeSection title="Deployment" tone="restrictions">

When deploying the Lone Wolf, choose one profile to deploy as the Lone Wolf. Additional Aspects deploy using the profile below. For each Aspect deployed, note publicly which profile is deployed in each location.

Aspects provide Orders as normal Troopers.

</CompanyTypeSection>
