## Ranking Up

When a unit gains enough XP during a round to advance to the next rank, it gains a new title, a single **Perk Point**, and occasionally a free skill.

Perk Points must be spent immediately. They cannot be saved for later ranks.

\* **Fireteam: Mercs** allows the unit to form Fireteams with other units that have this skill.

\** **Counts as Merc** means the unit counts as Merc when forming a Mercs Fireteam for pure bonuses.

## Perks

As units gain experience, they can translate that experience into tangible battlefield benefits. When a unit gains a rank, it gains **1 Perk Point** and must immediately spend it.

When spending a Perk Point, refer to the Perk trees and either select an eligible Perk or roll for a random Perk.

<RuleCard eyebrow="Perk Selection" title="Choosing a Perk">

To select a Perk directly, all of the following must be true:

1. The unit's rank is equal to or greater than the Perk's tier.
2. The unit has every directly connected lower-tier Perk leading to the chosen Perk.
3. The Perk has not already been selected.
4. The unit has the needed equipment slot, if the Perk grants gear or a slotted skill.

</RuleCard>

Connected lower-tier Perks may come from the original unit profile, equipment, previous Perk choices, or other rules. Some Perks grant gear or skills that fill slots, such as **360 Visor** as an Accessory or **Hacker** and **Doctor** as Roles.

If a unit does not have the needed slot, it may override an existing slot as normal. The unit receives no compensation for lost CR.

## Random Perks

Instead of selecting a Perk directly, a player may roll for a random Perk.

When rolling randomly, first roll to determine the Perk tree, then roll again on that tree. The player may select a legal Perk from the matching row.

Random Perks are slightly more flexible: a randomly rolled Perk may be **one tier higher** than the unit's current rank. If no Perk can be selected from the result, reroll on the table until a legal Perk can be selected.

<RuleCard eyebrow="Example" title="Rolling for a Perk">

A player chooses to roll instead of selecting a Perk. They roll a 4, which lets them select either **Initiative** or **Cool**. They choose **Initiative** and roll again.

The second roll is a 19, which would allow **MOV** or **Covering Fire**, but the trooper is only Rank 1. The player rerolls and gets an 11, allowing **Forward Deployment** or **Parachutist**. They select **Parachutist**.

</RuleCard>

## Skill Stacking

Skills do not stack unless a rule specifically says otherwise.

For example, if a trooper already has **Dodge (+3)**, selecting a Perk that grants **Dodge (+3)** does not increase that skill to **Dodge (+6)**.
