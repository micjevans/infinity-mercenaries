Infinity: Mercenaries is built around Companies: persistent mercenary teams that meet each other while completing contracts. Before a season begins, each player creates a Company.

The first step is choosing one or two sectorials for the Company. These represent the Captain's contacts and the pool of units the Company can recruit from. The two sectorials can come from different factions. Rivalries can be put aside for money.

## Company Types

Vanilla armies do **not** count as sectorials for company type requirements.

### Cohesive Company

<CompanyTypeSection title="Setup" tone="setup">

Select any **1 sectorial** and choose one Fireteam Core available to that sectorial. The Captain and all other purchased troopers must be able to join that fireteam.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Benefits" tone="benefits">

- All fireteam members are considered the same unit for fireteam bonuses.
- This Company may form 1 Fireteam Core from its sectorial with 6 or fewer Troopers.
- Gain +1 WIP at Fireteam Level 6.
- The Captain gains **FT Master**.
- All troopers gain **Number 2**.
- Ignore the Core Fireteam's minimum trooper restriction.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Restrictions" tone="restrictions">

- Tactical Awareness may never be spent on the Core Fireteam.
- Troopers purchased with Tactical Awareness are discounted by 3 CR.
- Troopers that are not part of the Core link are considered Irregular.

</CompanyTypeSection>

</CompanyTypeCard>

### Inspiring Leader

<CompanyTypeSection title="Setup" tone="setup">

Select any **1 sectorial** and choose a Captain from that sectorial.

Aside from the Captain, all other troopers must be Irregular troopers and may be purchased from any sectorial or vanilla army.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Benefits" tone="benefits">

The Captain gains **Inspiring Leadership**. Use any single sectorial's AVA for each trooper type without combining AVAs.

</CompanyTypeSection>

</CompanyTypeCard>

### Airborne Company

<CompanyTypeSection title="Setup" tone="setup">

Select any **1 sectorial** and choose a Captain from that sectorial.

Aside from the Captain, all other troopers must have an Airborne Deployment skill and may be purchased from any sectorial or vanilla army.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Benefits" tone="benefits">

The Captain gains **Parachutist** and **Network Support (Only Controlled Jump)**.

- The Company gets one free use of Speedball per contract, dropping only one Speedball.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Restrictions" tone="restrictions">

- Troopers in this Company must use an AD skill when deployed during the Deployment Phase.
- When deployed this way, troopers treat the opponent's Deployment Zone plus an additional 8 inches outside it as an Exclusion Zone.
- The Captain must be deployed during the Deployment Phase.

</CompanyTypeSection>

</CompanyTypeCard>

#### TAG Profile Customization

<CompanyTypeSection title="Customization" tone="notes">

When purchased, the TAG may use **20 Spec-Ops XP** to customize the profile using the Spec-Ops equipment and skills for the Company's sectorial.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Restrictions" tone="restrictions">

- ARM, BTS, and STR may not be modified using Spec-Ops XP.
- The TAG may be the Company's Captain, but does not gain additional Spec-Ops XP.
- The TAG may not purchase Forward Deployment, Strategic Deployment, Infiltration, Impersonation, Parachutist, Combat Jump, or Engineer with Spec-Ops XP.
- The TAG may never gain those skills by any other means.

</CompanyTypeSection>

#### Upgrading and Deploying a TAG

<CompanyTypeSection title="Progression" tone="benefits">

When a TAG receives enough experience to level up, it receives a TAG Perk in addition to the normally earned perk. The TAG Perk must be chosen from the TAG Perk Tree and may not be rolled randomly.

TAGs purchase and use equipment from markets like regular troopers for all slots except **Armor**.

</CompanyTypeSection>
<CompanyTypeSection title="Deployment" tone="restrictions">

When a TAG deploys in a contract, it takes up **two slots**. This means a Company may deploy only 3 total troopers for Elite Deployment and 5 total troopers for non-Elite Deployment if the TAG is included.

</CompanyTypeSection>

<CompanyTypeSection title="Aspect Rules" tone="notes">

The multiple profiles contribute to Company Renown, but the trooper's initial CR cost only counts once.

</CompanyTypeSection>

## The Captain

Every Company needs a leader. The Captain represents the main pillar of the Company.

To build a Captain, select a unit profile available to the chosen Sectorial Army or armies. The profile must be either a Spec-Ops unit or have the Lieutenant skill, following all recruitment restrictions.

Lieutenant special skill bonuses apply normally, such as +1 Order, +1 Command Token, and +1 SWC.

First, pay the unit's CR cost. Then spend **28 minus the unit's cost** as Spec-Ops XP to configure the Captain's loadout using one of the Spec-Ops charts available to the chosen sectorials.

The Captain also gains **Specialist Operative**.

Examples:

- Keisotsu Spec-Ops: 18 XP (28 - 10)
- Muyib Spec-Ops: 4 XP (28 - 24)
- Knight of Santiago: 0 XP

## Sponsor

When creating a Company, a player may optionally align with a Sponsor if the event organizer has announced any.

Sponsors often provide an initial benefit and further benefits as players accomplish missions. Players may only benefit from the Sponsor they are aligned with.

## Recruitment

Starting Credits are set by the organizer. Recommended starts are **60**, **75**, or **90 CR** depending on how strong teams should be at the beginning of the season. The online league uses **75 CR**.

Players may spend up to their starting CR to recruit units from the Company's sectorials. Treat a trooper's Infinity point value as its CR cost.

Recruitment restrictions:

- Characters and TAGs cannot be bought.
- Profiles with an SWC cost cannot be bought.
- When purchasing a profile with Transmutation (Auto), choose only one form. The use of Transmutation (Auto) is banned.
- The number of units purchased over the life of a Company, including the Captain, cannot exceed their own AVA.
- If a unit is listed in both chosen sectorials, use the higher AVA.

### Peripheral Recruitment

When purchasing Peripherals for a Company:

- A Peripheral (Servant) must be associated with another unit in the Company that has Doctor or Engineer.
- The Peripheral (Servant) is considered equipment, takes no slot, and is part of the Doctor/Engineer loadout.
- The Peripheral counts toward its owner's Renown.
- When purchasing Peripheral Controller combos, only one Peripheral may be purchased per Controller. Subtract the cost of excess Peripherals that are not gained.

Example: A Chimera purchased with three Pupniks only gains one Pupnik. The player pays 17 CR for the pair instead of 19 CR by subtracting the cost of the two unused 1-point Pupniks.

## Renown

Renown measures how much attention and strength a Company has accumulated.

A Company's Renown is calculated by adding together the Renown of each unit in the Company, plus any additional Renown gained by the Company.

A unit's Renown is the sum of:

- The unit's initial cost.
- The base cost of all equipment in the unit's loadout.
- The unit's XP.

For event organizers, Renown is an important number to track. It contributes to Inducements during contracts, may affect round pairings, and can be used as a win condition.

## Notoriety

Notoriety represents how certain factions view the Company. Criminal or illegal choices often increase Notoriety, while lawful choices may decrease it.

Notoriety primarily affects equipment costs through War Market alignment.
