Infinity: Mercenaries is built around Companies: persistent mercenary teams that grow over a season of contracts.

Before a season begins, each player creates one Company.

Choose one or two sectorials for the Company. These represent the Captain's contacts and define the unit pool your Company can recruit from. The two sectorials can come from different factions.

## Company Types

Company types are the broad framework your Company is built around. They shape how you assemble the roster, what bonuses the Company gains, and what restrictions it must follow.

Some company types are flexible and general-purpose. Others trade that flexibility for a stronger theme, a special benefit, or a more demanding recruitment structure.

## Sponsors

Sponsors are in-world corporations that take notice of mercenary Companies as a Season progresses. Each sponsor has a specific objective — usually tied to a style of play or a piece of equipment — and tracks each player's individual progress toward that objective over the course of the event.

During Company creation, each player may align with one Sponsor if Sponsors are available for the event. A player may only receive benefits from their chosen Sponsor.

### How Sponsors Work

Each sponsor defines an objective and a set of milestones. As a player accomplishes the sponsor's objective during Contracts, they record their progress and report it to the organizer. When cumulative progress reaches a milestone, the player unlocks the associated benefit immediately.

Milestone thresholds typically are achieved solo by the player but in rare instances an event may have collective objectives aligned to that sponsor.

### Sponsor Benefits

Each sponsor offers a tiered set of benefits that unlock as milestones are reached. Early tiers tend to offer small discounts or minor upgrades, while later tiers unlock more powerful or unique options. Each sponsor entry describes exactly what is offered at each tier and any conditions that apply to receiving it.

## The Captain

Every Company needs a leader. The Captain is the core of the Company.

To build a Captain, select a unit profile available to your chosen sectorial or sectorials. The profile must be either:

- A Spec-Ops unit, or
- A profile with the Lieutenant skill.

All normal recruitment restrictions still apply.

## Recruitment

Starting Credits are set by the organizer. Recommended starting values are **60**, **75**, or **90 CR**, depending on desired campaign power level. The online league uses **75 CR**.

Players may spend up to their starting CR to recruit units from the Company's sectorials. Treat a trooper's Infinity points value as its CR cost.

  title="Other Starting CR Uses"

  <p>Initial CR does not have to be spent on recruitment alone. Players may also spend starting CR in other valid ways, such as buying equipment or purchasing services.</p>

Recruitment restrictions:

- Characters and TAGs cannot be recruited.
- Profiles with an SWC cost cannot be recruited.
- For profiles with Transmutation (Auto), choose only one form at purchase. Transmutation (Auto) is otherwise banned.
- Over the life of the Company, the number of purchased copies of a unit (including the Captain if applicable) cannot exceed that unit's AVA.
- If a unit is listed in both chosen sectorials, use the higher AVA value.

### Peripheral Recruitment

When purchasing Peripherals for a Company:

- A Peripheral (Servant) must be associated with another unit in the Company that has Doctor or Engineer.
- The Peripheral (Servant/Ancillary) is considered equipment, takes no slot, and is part of the Doctor/Engineer loadout.
- The Peripheral (Servant/Ancillary) counts toward its owner's Renown.
- When purchasing Peripheral Controller combos, only one Peripheral may be purchased per Controller. Subtract the cost of excess Peripherals that are not gained.

<ExampleCallout>
  <p>A Chimera purchased with three Pupniks only gains one Pupnik. The player pays 17 CR for the pair instead of 19 CR, subtracting the cost of the two unused 1-point Pupniks.</p>
</ExampleCallout>

## Renown

Renown measures how much attention and power a Company has accumulated.

<p className="rules-formula"><code>Company Renown = total Unit Renown + any additional Company Renown (winning contracts)</code></p>

<p className="rules-formula"><code>Unit Renown = initial cost + base cost of equipped items + unit XP</code></p>

For event organizers, Renown is an important number to track as it is a rough indicator of how strong a Company is. It contributes to Inducements during contracts, may affect round pairings, and can be used as an events win condition.

## Notoriety

Notoriety represents how factions perceive the Company. Criminal or illegal choices often increase Notoriety, while lawful choices may decrease it.

Notoriety may matter in other parts of campaign play, but it primarily affects equipment costs through War Market alignment.
