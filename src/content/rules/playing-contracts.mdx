## Contract Overview

Each round is centered around the **Contract**. A Contract is a modified mission where players face off using their mercenary Companies and the Infinity ruleset by Corvus Belli.

All Contracts designed by the Mercenaries development team are intended to be played on a **32 by 48 inch** game table. Games use the full Infinity ruleset with the Mercenaries adjustments layered on top.

## Classified Objectives

At the start of each Contract, players receive **two Classified Objectives**. One may be chosen from the deck, while the second must be randomly drawn.

Classified Objectives are always used unless a Contract specifically says otherwise. They may contribute to scoring, grant small buffs, or reward players for completing side objectives.

When Classified Objectives are used, each player places an HVT following the normal Infinity HVT rules. If both players deploy on the same side of the table, both HVTs must be placed **at least 4 inches from the center line**, on the side of the table opposite the deployment zone.

## Inducements

Corporations are always watching their hirelings. When a Contract looks lopsided, they may offer additional temporary resources to the underdog to make the job worth finishing.

When players are paired with different deployed Renown totals, the player with lower deployed Renown receives Inducements before the Contract begins. Cooperative Contracts do not provide Inducements for Renown difference.

Inducements are temporary funds. They can only be spent on benefits for the current Contract, and anything purchased with Inducements disappears at the end of that Contract before Downtime.

<RuleCard eyebrow="Formula" title="Calculating Inducements">

Take half the difference between the players' deployed Renown totals, then round down to the nearest 5.

For example, if Jack deploys **90 Renown** and Jill deploys **143 Renown**, the difference is 53. Half of that is 26.5, which rounds down to **25 Inducements**.

</RuleCard>

## Deploying a Company

When selecting troopers for a Contract, a Company may deploy a maximum of **6 troopers**. One of those troopers must be the **Captain**.

The Captain is considered the list's Lieutenant and gains the Lieutenant Order as normal. Troopers should be selected before players meet for the Contract, following the same list-building process as a standard game of Infinity.

## Elite Deployment

<p class="company-type-tagline">Deploy light, hit hard, and trade board presence for elite efficiency bonuses.</p>

<CompanyTypeSection title="Restrictions" tone="restrictions">

- A Company is **Elite Deployed** only when it deploys the Captain and no more than **3 additional troopers**.
- Elite Deployment is determined by models deployed for that specific Contract, not total roster size.

</CompanyTypeSection>

<CompanyTypeSection title="Benefits" tone="benefits">

- Non-Captain troopers gain **Tactical Awareness**. If a unit already has Tactical Awareness, it gains no additional benefit.
- The Captain's injury-healing ability (see [The Captain](/rules/the-company/)) may be used on a trooper other than the Captain for that Contract.
- All troopers deployed this way gain an additional **+3 XP**, in addition to normal deployment XP.

</CompanyTypeSection>

At the end of every Contract, every unit that entered the **Dead** state must make a save roll against **PH − 3**. A failed roll results in a permanent Injury. See [Injuries and Recovery](/rules/injuries-and-recovery/) for the full procedure and injury table.

## Bye Rounds

Occasionally, a player may be unable to play a round or an event may have an odd number of players. In that case, the player receives a **bye round**.

A player with a bye round gains the normal rewards for the round, plus **12 CR** as if they had achieved 3 Objective Points.

To make up for the lost opportunity to gain XP, select up to **6 units** in the roster. Each selected unit gains **5 XP**.
