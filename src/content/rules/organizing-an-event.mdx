## Season Overview

A Mercenaries event is usually run as a series of Contracts called a **Season**. Players should create a new Company at the start of a Season, or all begin with Companies of similar strength.

During a Season, players are paired into Contracts. Each Contract is a game of Infinity played with both players' mercenary Companies. Between Contracts, Companies resolve downtime, rank up units, purchase equipment, recruit new troops, and deal with the consequences of earlier games.

At the end of a Season, a winner is often declared. The simplest win condition is highest Renown, but the organizer may define a different goal.

## Organizer Responsibilities

To organize an event, choose a series of Contracts for players to play during the Season. Mercenaries Contracts are custom missions designed for this format.

After choosing the Contracts, each player creates their Company. When the event begins, the organizer creates pairings before each round. Pairings may be narrative-driven, round robin, Swiss-style, or any other structure that fits the event.

1. Choose the number of rounds.
2. Choose the Contract pool.
3. Decide whether sponsors are in use.
4. Set starting CR and any event-specific restrictions.
5. Have players create Companies.
6. Pair players each round.
7. Track results, Renown, sponsor progress, and any organizer-defined win condition.

## Sponsors

Sponsors are a thematic way to show how in-world corporations take notice of mercenary Companies. When sponsors enter the Mercenaries sphere, they offer benefits in exchange for work. They do not care who performs the work, only that it gets done.

When creating an event, the organizer may optionally select one or more sponsors. **Two sponsors** is the recommended default for a full Season.

Each sponsor has several tiers of objectives and an associated reward for each tier. Players unlock these rewards by collectively working toward sponsor objectives, tracking their individual progress during Contracts, and reporting that progress to the organizer.

Announce sponsors before the start of the Season so players can consider the rewards when creating their Companies.

## Rounds

Every round in a Season represents a period of in-world time from the start of one Contract to just before the start of the next Contract.

A round usually includes:

- Playing a Contract.
- Gaining XP, CR, and other rewards.
- Resolving injuries and recovery.
- Resolving downtime events.
- Ranking up units.
- Purchasing equipment, services, staff, or recruits.

These are the standard steps, but organizers are encouraged to add narrative elements or event-specific activities if they improve the experience.

## Contracts

Each round is centered around a Contract. A Contract is a modified mission where players face off using their mercenary Companies and the Infinity ruleset by Corvus Belli.

Mercenaries Contracts designed by the development team are intended to be played on a **32 by 48 inch** game table.

Contracts should be chosen with the event's format in mind. A long Season can support more unusual missions and narrative variance. A short event should use clearer, faster Contracts with fewer edge-case rules.

## One-Day Event Format

Mercenaries can also be run as a one-day event in a more standard tournament format. This is best treated as a compressed Season, not as a replacement for the normal long-form campaign.

The goal is to give players a complete arc in **3 or 4 rounds** while limiting snowball effects and administrative overhead.

<WarningCallout title="Compressed Season Balance">

Short events magnify every reward, injury, and pairing result. Keep progression faster than a normal event, but avoid adding so many rewards that the final round becomes wildly uneven.

</WarningCallout>

### Balance Recommendations

For a compressed Season, use these adjustments unless the organizer has a specific narrative reason to do otherwise.

- Start all Companies at the same CR and do not allow pre-event XP advantages.
- Use Contracts with clear scoring and limited bookkeeping.
- Keep downtime short. Resolve one downtime event after each round except the final round.
- Preselect War Markets or other purchasing options before the event starts.
- Use Inducements normally to help lower-Renown Companies remain competitive.
- Do not allow players to make purchases after the final round unless the event continues later.
- Pair by Objective Points first, then Renown or strength of schedule as a tiebreaker.
- If prizes are involved, reward sportsmanship, narrative moments, or sponsor contribution alongside final standing.

### Accelerated Progression

Short events need enough progression to feel like Mercenaries, but not so much that the event becomes unstable.

For a **3-round event**, consider:

- 90 starting CR.
- Normal XP gain.
- Normal CR gain, plus a flat **+10 CR** after rounds 1 and 2.
- One downtime event after rounds 1 and 2 only.
- Sponsor milestones reduced by roughly half.

For a **4-round event**, consider:

- 75 or 90 starting CR.
- Normal XP gain.
- Normal CR gain.
- One downtime event after rounds 1, 2, and 3.
- Sponsor milestones reduced by roughly one third.

### Contract Selection

Choose Contracts that let players finish reliably. For one-day events, avoid stacking too many missions with heavy PvE, complex custom objects, or long post-game resolution.

A good compressed Season Contract pool should include:

- One straightforward objective-control Contract.
- One movement or extraction Contract.
- One combat-forward Contract.
- One optional narrative or set-piece Contract for a 4-round event.

### Time Management

Set round times before the event starts. A practical structure is:

- 10-15 minutes for pairings and table assignment.
- 90-120 minutes for each Contract.
- 15-20 minutes after each round for reporting, rewards, purchases, and downtime.
- 30-45 minutes for a meal break during a 4-round event.

If players are new to Mercenaries, choose the longer end of each range.
