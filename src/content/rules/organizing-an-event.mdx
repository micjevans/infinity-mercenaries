## Season Overview

This page is written for the person running the event. It covers how a Season is structured, what decisions the organizer makes before play begins, and how to run a shorter one-day format if needed. Players looking for rules that apply during play should refer to the individual rules pages instead.

A Mercenaries event is usually run as a series of Contracts called a **Season**. Players create a new Company at the start of a Season, or all begin with Companies of similar strength.

During a Season, players are paired into Contracts. Each Contract is a game of Infinity played with both players' mercenary Companies. Between Contracts, Companies resolve downtime, rank up units, purchase equipment, recruit new troops, and deal with the consequences of earlier games.

At the end of a Season, a winner is often declared. The simplest win condition is highest Renown, but the organizer may define a different goal.

## Organizer Responsibilities

To run a Season, the organizer selects the Contracts, sets the starting conditions, and manages pairings and progression throughout the event. Players handle the rest. Pairings may be narrative-driven, round robin, Swiss-style, or any other structure that fits.

## Sponsors

When setting up a Season, the organizer decides whether Sponsors are in use and which ones players may choose from. Sponsors are written in pairs so **Two Sponsors** is a good default for a Season however organizers may choose to allow any number or all if they wish.

Announce the available Sponsors before Company creation begins so players can factor the rewards into how they build their Companies.

By default, each player individually tracks their own progress toward their chosen Sponsor's milestones. Two organizer-controlled variants are also available:

- **Limited pool.** Often the organizer may only offer a subset of available Sponsors rather than all of them. This can focus the event narrative.
- **Collective contribution.** In rare cases, all players aligned with a Sponsor may contribute progress toward shared milestone thresholds rather than tracking individually. This creates a cooperative dynamic but increases organizer bookkeeping and can make rewards feel less personal.

## Starting Conditions

Before Company creation begins, the organizer sets the starting CR. All players must use the same value.

Use **75 CR** if you are unsure. It is the most playtested value and keeps the first few rounds competitive regardless of faction choice.

## Rounds

Every round in a Season represents a period of in-world time from the start of one Contract to just before the start of the next Contract.

A round usually includes:

- Playing a Contract.
- Gaining XP, CR, and other rewards.
- Resolving injuries and recovery.
- Resolving downtime events.
- Ranking up units.
- Purchasing equipment, services, staff, or recruits.

These are the standard steps, but organizers are encouraged to add narrative elements or event-specific activities if they improve the experience.

## Contracts

Each round is centered around a Contract. A Contract is a modified mission where players face off using their mercenary Companies and the Infinity ruleset by Corvus Belli.

Mercenaries Contracts designed by the development team are intended to be played on a **32 by 48 inch** game table.

Contracts should be chosen with the event's format in mind. A long Season can support more unusual missions and narrative variance. A short event should use clearer, faster Contracts with fewer edge-case rules.

## One-Day Event Format

Mercenaries can also be run as a one-day event in a more standard tournament format. This is best treated as a compressed Season, not as a replacement for the normal long-form campaign.

The goal is to give players a complete arc in **3 or 4 rounds** while limiting snowball effects and administrative overhead.

<WarningCallout title="Compressed Season Balance">

Short events magnify every reward, injury, and pairing result. Keep progression faster than a normal event, but avoid adding so many rewards that the final round becomes wildly uneven.

</WarningCallout>
