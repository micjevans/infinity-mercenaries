## Injury Rolls

The mercenary life is dangerous, and injuries are common. At the end of a Contract, every unit that entered the **Dead** state must make a save roll against **PH − 3** (roll equal to or under the unit's PH − 3 on a d20).

If the roll succeeds, the unit is back on its feet, wheels, or other applicable locomotion with no further consequences. If the roll fails, the unit rolls on the Injury table.

When a unit gains an Injury, write it on the unit's roster entry and apply the listed attribute or rule change.

<RuleCard eyebrow="Injury Limit" title="Each Injury Is Unique">

If the same Injury is rolled twice for a unit, reroll until a new result can be applied.

If a unit already has every Injury in the table, no additional Injury roll is needed.

</RuleCard>

## Recovery

<RuleCard eyebrow="Recovery Rule" title="Rest and Recuperation">

Only time and rest can heal a unit. If a unit is **not deployed** during a Contract, remove one of that unit's Injuries at the end of that match.

Bye rounds count as not being deployed. All units on the roster receive the normal recovery benefit when their player receives a bye.

</RuleCard>

<RuleCard eyebrow="Recovery Rule" title="Captain's Resilience">

Captains do not have the luxury of sitting idle. A Captain may remove a single Injury at the end of each Contract, even if the Captain was deployed.

</RuleCard>
